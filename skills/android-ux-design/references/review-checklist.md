# Review Checklist

Use this before handoff.

## Direction

- Is the direction named and coherent?
- Does the app feel like one product?
- Does anything feel like generic mobile template output?

## Platform Fit

- Does the screen feel Android-native?
- Does navigation follow Android expectations?
- Does back behavior make sense?
- Are system bars and edge-to-edge handled cleanly?

## Hierarchy

- Is the primary action obvious?
- Is the information density appropriate for a handheld screen?
- Are labels and grouping clear?

## Layout

- Is thumb reach reasonable for common actions?
- Does the layout work on compact and larger devices?
- Is extra space on tablets or foldables used meaningfully?

## Typography

- Is the type hierarchy clear?
- Are small labels still readable?
- Does text scale reasonably for accessibility?

## Color + Theme

- Are theme roles used consistently?
- Is contrast strong enough?
- Is dynamic color policy intentional rather than accidental?

## Components

- Do buttons, fields, lists, and sheets feel credible?
- Are touch targets large enough?
- Are hover, focus, pressed, disabled, and error states handled where relevant?

## States

- Loading state present?
- Empty state present?
- Error state present?
- Offline or retry behavior handled if relevant?

## Accessibility

- Keyboard, TalkBack, and focus behavior considered?
- Contrast acceptable?
- Motion restrained and reducible?
- Labels, semantics, and affordances clear?

## Final Question

- Would a strong Android designer or engineer believe this UI was made for Android on purpose?
