# Onafriq Slide Brand Rules

Use this summary when building or editing Onafriq slides from `onafriq-slides-template.pptx`.

For authoritative detail, see `onafriq-brand-guide-v4.pdf`.

## Brand intent and tone

- Purpose: unlock access to a borderless world.
- Personality: proactive, enlightening, influential, considered.
- Tone of voice: accessible, knowledgeable, inspiring, resonant, ardent.
- Slide copy should feel clear, informed, confident, and professional.

## Typography

- Brand fonts: `Radikal` first choice, `Century Gothic` fallback
- Use the font family and type hierarchy already present in the template.
- Keep headline, subheadline, and body relationships intact.
- Rewrite or split content before compressing type beyond the template's visual logic.

## Color palette

Primary palette from the brand guide:

- White: `RGB 255 255 255`
- Charcoal: `RGB 47 50 53`
- Yellow: `RGB 240 240 0`
- Blue: `RGB 59 59 255`
- Orange: `RGB 255 127 0`

Usage guidance:

- Default to the colors already used in the template.
- Keep logo usage within approved treatments:
  - white on charcoal
  - charcoal on white
  - charcoal on yellow
  - yellow on charcoal
- Do not put the yellow logo on white or light imagery.
- Do not recolor the logo with secondary colors.

## Logo handling

- Use only provided logo artwork.
- Do not redraw, distort, warp, shear, rotate, outline, or add effects.
- Maintain clear space around the logo.
- On layouts, logo placement should stay where the template puts it; top-left and bottom-left are standard brand positions.
- On images, place the logo only where the image is quiet enough and contrast remains strong.

## Layout system

- The brand relies on a strong grid and disciplined margins.
- The curved holding device is part of the Onafriq visual system; preserve it where the template uses it.
- Keep generous clear space around type and graphic elements.
- For slides, preserve the exact structure of the chosen template slide instead of rebuilding the composition from scratch.

## Photography

- Prefer imagery centered on people in Africa connecting, transacting, or benefiting from mobile financial solutions.
- Favor close crops over distant wide shots.
- Choose images that feel positive, human, authentic, and contextually African.
- Avoid busy areas behind the logo.

## Pattern and iconography

- The brand pattern comes from tight crops of distinctive Onafriq logo characters.
- Use patterns only when the template already uses them or when matching an existing brand composition.
- Keep iconography consistent and simple; do not introduce an unrelated icon style.

## Slide-making implications

- Start from the template deck, never from a blank page.
- Duplicate the closest source slide before editing.
- Keep the built-in footer, logo, and page number treatment.
- Fit content to the layout; do not break the brand system to make copy fit.
- Delete unused template slides from the final deck.
